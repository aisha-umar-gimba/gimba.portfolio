About Me

I am a consultant, lawyer and columnist from Yobe State, practising in Abuja. I specialize in legislative and public policy advocacy, negotiation, and disaster management across the West and Central African region. Out of passion, I advocate for cultural heritage preservation and resuscitation of literature and arts in the north. In 2015, I cofounded the Yobe Literary Society (YLS) to turn passion into reality. I encourage dialogue as a way of formulating policies and solving societal ills. I curated the first book and arts festival in the northeast – the Yobe Peace and Arts Festival (YOPAFEST) in 2021...

the same year, I published his first book "The Howls on the Highway to Hell" and co-authored the Society’s first book "iWrite: an Essential Guide to Writing in the 21st Century". I am the curator of TEDxBazeUniversity. In 2022, I edited the Society’s second book “Journey to a Future-Past: Yobe through the Times”, written by Yahuza Rabi’u Garba. I have worked with Hexalix Properties Ltd as a Legal Officer, the Civil Society Legislative Advocacy Centre (CISLAC, which serves as the National Chapter of Transparency International) and Médecins Sans Frontières West and Central Africa as Ambassador Assistant. I have worked on various committees and consulted for numerous organizations. In 2021, I worked with the African Commission on Human and the National Human Rights Commission as part of a strategic consultation to put a report on declarations of state of emergency and disaster laws in Africa to the African Union. I worked with the House of Representatives Adhoc Committee on Real Estate to check the excesses of developers in the Federal Capital Territory and helped formulate policies to that effect. I also served on the Yobe Stare Committee on the Yobe Education Trust Fund (YETFund) and registered it with the Corporate Affairs Commission. I was a guest at the Kaduna Book and Arts Festival in 2022. I love to play chess and analyze football matches in my free time.
